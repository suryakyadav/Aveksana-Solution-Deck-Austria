The problem

Two failure points throttle every research institution.

Universities and funding ecosystems share the same hidden bottleneck: research output is gated not by ideas, but by two structural friction points — and both compound silently across every cohort, every funding round.

01
The completion gap
Students delay or abandon theses. Supervisors are overloaded. Doctoral pipelines stall. Every cohort that finishes late drags the next one with it.
02
The funding gap
Researchers and SMEs spend 100+ hours per proposal at 14–17% success rates. The slowest phase — absorbing rules, structuring the first draft — is where most projects quietly die.

Aveksana is one platform built to remove both.

Why this matters in Austria

The Leistungsvereinbarung rewards exactly what Aveksana improves.

Austrian public universities are steered through three-year performance agreements with the BMBWF. Within the three-pillar funding model, a defined set of competition indicators directly shapes each university's negotiating position for the next budget period.

Performance-agreement KPI What moves it Aveksana
On-time graduations Thesis completion, supervision capacity, research-method readiness ✓ Thesis module
Students with 40+ ECTS / academic year Reduced friction in research-heavy courses; better study throughput ✓ Thesis module
Doctoral programme throughput PhD methods support; structured proposal & thesis scaffolding ✓ Both modules
Third-party funding raised (Drittmittel) More proposals submitted; higher quality per proposal ✓ Artha module

Same logic applies to Fachhochschulen (place allocation, accreditation throughput) and to FFG/COMET-funded research consortia (bid quality, win rate, doctoral participation).

Module 1 · Thesis Success

From first research course to defended thesis.

Most "AI for students" tools optimize for writing speed. Aveksana optimizes for research capability — adapting to where each student is, scaffolding what they actually need, and giving supervisors back the time they lose to repetition.

  1. Research Learning Tool

    AI-guided research fundamentals. Hands-on exercises that prepare students before thesis writing — so the thesis isn't the first time they've done real research.

  2. Methods Course Assistant

    For graduate programs and research-method courses. Practical application of methods through structured project workflows.

  3. AI-Powered Thesis Writing

    Topic selection, literature framing, structuring. Reduces supervisor workload — directly improves on-time completion.

For supervisors

Less repetition. More substance.

Supervisors stop re-explaining structure, citation logic, and method choice. They engage with ideas, not formatting.

For doctoral schools

Pipeline that doesn't stall.

Methods scaffolding for PhD candidates aligns directly with GUEP 2025–2030 emphasis on doctoral training.

Module 2 · Artha

Funding applications, accelerated — for researchers and for SMEs.

Artha is a horizontal funding-application platform. The same engine helps a Horizon Europe coordinator, an FWF principal investigator, and an FFG-applying SME — because the underlying problem is the same: 100+ hours absorbing rules and producing a structured first draft.

  1. Idea Validator

    Research Gap Score (0–100%) — instant feedback on novelty and fundability.

  2. Proposal Builder

    Structured first draft in ~20 minutes, aligned with funder review criteria.

  3. Reviewer Insights

    Optimize each proposal against the targeted agency's preferences and criteria.

  4. Funding Match

    Personalized grant recommendations matched to applicant and project (rolling out).

The shared engine · R&D depth

Why this isn't just another LLM wrapper.

Most "AI for research" tools are general-purpose models with a research-themed prompt. Aveksana is built on two layers competitors don't have — and it's this combination that makes the platform applied research, not a SaaS feature.

Layer 01

Multi-theoretical pedagogical engine

A synthesis of five learning theories — learner autonomy, inquiry-based learning, scaffolded support, metacognitive development, constructivist foundations — operationalized as adaptive scaffolding that responds to where each user is in their research development.

The pedagogy makes it a teacher, not a tool.

Layer 02

Workflow-specific AI for research production

Tuned to the actual stages of research output — idea validation → literature grounding → proposal architecture → thesis structuring → reviewer alignment — rather than treating every task as generic text generation.

The workflow specificity is where general AI hallucinates and we don't.

This combined moat — pedagogy and workflow — is what makes Aveksana relevant to applied-research funders (FFG, CDG, COMET) as a research-grade innovation, not just a commercial product.
